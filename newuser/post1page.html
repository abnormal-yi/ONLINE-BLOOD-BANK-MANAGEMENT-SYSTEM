ABOUT DODOMA DAMU SALAMA

Dodoma damu salama

In Dodoma Region, blood collection, safety and management is an activity that is carried out by Dodoma Damu Salama branch of Tanzania National Blood Transfusion Service (NBTS) under the Ministry of Health (MOH), mandated to collect blood from voluntary non remurated blood donors (VNRBDs). The program was established in line with World Health Assembly resolution (WHA) in 1972, which argues all member states including Tanzania to devolop complehensive and well cordinated blood transfusion service based on voluntary and non-remunerated blood donors. NTBS was founded in 2004 through cooperative Agreement (COAG) between USG and government of Tanzania (GOT), through support of PEPFAR funding. The first five years COAG which ended in march 2010, focused mainly on emergeny establishment of blood transfusion program while the second five-year COAG (2010 - 15) is mainly focusing on NBTS strengthening and suistainability which ended in April, 2015 and GOT took over all operation cost a Human resourse management.

THE DONNATION OF BLOOD IN DODODMA

Short Summary About Blood Donation In Dodoma

In the Dodma region, the demand for blood is still much higher than the actual consumption. Statistics show that the annual blood supply is less than the demand for blood in Dodoma region. So we still need a lot of blood donors to meet the needs of Dodoma residents.

BLOOD REQURED IN DODOMA

The Blood required in Dodoma

Dodoma Region uses 2,000 liters of blood for different groups of blood donors per month. This will be distributed to different hospitals throughout the Dodoma region, where a large percentage will be used by pregnant women and children under the age of five.
